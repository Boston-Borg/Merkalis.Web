Our thesis

Integrity should be a property of the structure — not an audit you run later, or a report you generate at the end.

— The principle behind every line of Kastoria.

The problem

Three structural failures of healthcare data today.

Healthcare didn't lose its data integrity by accident. It was built that way. Three architectural choices, made decades ago, are now the gating risk for every AI initiative on the table.

Failure 01

Overwrite-First Architecture

Clinical systems routinely overwrite records as a normal operation. Audit logs sit separate from the data itself — so logs diverge from actual state, and "what we did" stops matching "what we have."

"Logs separate from data can be altered. Lineage embedded in the data graph cannot."

Failure 02

The Data Duplication Tax

AI and research teams spend months physically copying terabytes of clinical data just to assemble a governed dataset. Every copy drifts from source. Every copy opens a new lineage gap. Every copy is a new breach surface.

Provenance shouldn't cost a month of engineering per project.

Failure 03

Vendor Lock-In Gravity

Data trapped in proprietary systems is data you can't move. Migrations require costly re-ingestion. Every year of inertia raises the switching cost — and every replacement decision starts with a six-figure data tax.

Storage formats outlive the vendors who chose them. They should outlive the lock-in, too.

What Kastoria does

Nine functions. One verifiable record.

The seed lands here — and grows into Kastoria's nine core functions. Foundation at the top. Operations in the middle. The capabilities that make Kastoria different at the bottom.

01 / Ingest

Take in everything.

Multimodal by design — imaging, EHR, genomics, free text, telemetry, wearables. One pipeline, every format, interconnected by patient and encounter IDs.

02 / Govern

Decide who sees what.

Policy-driven access control down to the group, item, and projection level. Defensible to your compliance officer on the first ask.

03 / Verify

Prove what you used.

Cryptographic integrity by default. Every record is content-addressed and Merkle-verified — court-admissible evidence by construction, not by separate report.

04 / Replicate

Run anywhere, stay in sync.

Hybrid and multi-cloud, CRDT-backed, eventually consistent. Move across regions or storage providers without re-indexing.

05 / Process

Build pipelines without lock-in.

Customizable WebAssembly pipelines. Event-driven hooks let models attach directly to ingest streams — no ETL, no vendor reshuffle.

06 / Operationalize

AI-ready, audit-ready.

Versioned, forkable, hash-verified. The same record powers your model and your auditor — no extra reconciliation work.

07 / Zero-Copy AI

Train without duplicating.

Virtual projections — de-identified, performance-optimized views of the same record. No physical copying. Full lineage from training data to inference call.

08 / Immutable History

Every version preserved.

Every mutation, every processing event stored in the repository. Lineage embedded in the data graph — so "what changed and when" stops being a forensic project.

09 / Verifiable Deletion

Forget completely. Prove it.

GDPR right-to-be-forgotten done properly — deletion propagates provably to every linked de-identified copy. Compliance defends with math, not a memo.
